# AI in Healthcare: Market Landscape 2026

## Key Takeaway

The diagnostic AI market is consolidating fast. Three players now control 60% of FDA-cleared algorithms. If we're entering this space, we need to partner — not build from scratch.

## Market Size

- **2025:** $4.2B (up 34% YoY)
- **2026 projected:** $5.8B
- **2030 projected:** $18.5B (CAGR ~28%)
- Fastest-growing segment: radiology AI ($1.9B, 41% of total)

## Trends Worth Watching

**1. Ambient documentation is exploding.** Nuance (Microsoft) owns the hospital market, but startups like Abridge and Nabla are winning smaller clinics. Doctors save ~2 hours/day on notes. This is the easiest sell in healthcare AI right now.

**2. Payer-side AI is underrated.** Prior authorization automation is a $12B problem. Most startups focus on providers — the insurance side has less competition and faster sales cycles.

**3. Regulation is becoming a moat.** FDA's new predetermined change control plan means companies with existing clearances can update models without re-filing. Late entrants face 12-18 month approval timelines that incumbents skip entirely.

## Competitive Gaps

- **Mental health:** No dominant AI player. Woebot pivoted, Wysa is small. Huge unmet demand.
- **Dental AI:** Pearl and Overjet are early but the market is fragmented across 130K independent practices. Distribution is the hard part.
- **Clinical trials matching:** Most solutions are keyword-based. An LLM-native approach could 10x patient matching accuracy.

## Recommendation

Skip diagnostic imaging — it's a red ocean. The best entry points are:

1. **Ambient documentation for specialists** (dermatology, ophthalmology — underserved by Nuance)
2. **Prior auth automation** targeting mid-size payers
3. **Clinical trial matching** as a wedge into health systems
